About NVIDIA

NVIDIA is an American technology company that designs graphics processing units (GPUs), artificial intelligence hardware, and advanced computing technologies. The company was founded in 1993 by Jensen Huang, Chris Malachowsky, and Curtis Priem. It is widely known for its powerful graphics cards used in gaming, professional visualization, and artificial intelligence systems.

Over the years, NVIDIA has become one of the leading companies in the semiconductor industry. Its technologies power gaming computers, data centers, robotics, autonomous vehicles, and scientific research. Today, NVIDIA is considered one of the most important companies driving the growth of artificial intelligence and accelerated computing worldwide.

NVIDIA Headquartars

NVIDIA has its global headquarters in Santa Clara. The corporate campus is located at 2788 San Tomas Expressway, Santa Clara, California.

he headquarters campus includes modern office buildings designed with advanced architecture and sustainable features. The main buildings are called Voyager and Endeavor, and their triangular design reflects the triangular geometry used in computer graphics rendering.

The campus provides workspaces, research labs, and collaboration areas for engineers and developers who work on NVIDIA’s GPU and AI technologies.

Founders

NVIDIA was founded in 1993 by three engineers who had a vision to transform computer graphics and computing technology. The founders believed that graphics processing would become very important for gaming, multimedia, and scientific computing.

Jensen Huang

Jensen Huang is one of the co-founders and the current Chief Executive Officer (CEO) of NVIDIA. He has led the company since its founding and played a major role in developing GPUs and expanding NVIDIA into artificial intelligence, data centers, and advanced computing technologies.

Chris Malachowsky

Chris Malachowsky is a co-founder and a computer engineer who helped design NVIDIA’s early graphics processing units. He contributed to many important GPU technologies and has been involved in research and engineering leadership at the company.

Curtis Priem

Curtis Priem is the third co-founder of NVIDIA and was responsible for developing early graphics chip architectures. His work helped lay the foundation for NVIDIA’s first successful graphics processors.

Major Products

NVIDIA develops a wide range of hardware and software products used in gaming, artificial intelligence, professional graphics, and data centers. Some of the major NVIDIA products include the following

1. GeForce RTX Series

The GeForce RTX series is NVIDIA’s most popular line of graphics cards for gaming and creative work. These GPUs provide high performance, real-time ray tracing, and AI-powered graphics. They are widely used by gamers, video editors, and designers.

2. NVIDIA A100

The NVIDIA A100 is a powerful GPU designed for data centers and artificial intelligence workloads. It is commonly used for machine learning, deep learning, and large-scale computing tasks.

3. NVIDIA H100

The NVIDIA H100 is an advanced AI GPU used for training and running large AI models. It provides high performance for AI research, cloud computing, and enterprise applications.

4. NVIDIA CUDA

CUDA is a parallel computing platform and programming model created by NVIDIA. It allows developers to use GPUs to perform complex calculations faster than traditional CPUs.

5. NVIDIA Omniverse

NVIDIA Omniverse is a platform for 3D design collaboration, simulation, and digital twins. It is used by designers, engineers, and developers to create and simulate virtual environments.
